We are Marine Ecophysiology and Biogeochemistry (MEB)!

Our research group primarily focuses on the role of trace metals in the marine biogeochemical cycling of carbon, nitrogen, and phosphorus, the physiological and ecological responses of marine phytoplankton to global changes and their mechanisms, as well as the study of the marine ecotoxicological effects and mechanisms of heavy metals and organic pollutants.

Academic Progress

  • Our team published results in Science Advances, revealing the nutrient regulation mechanism of biological nitrogen fixation in the Northwest Pacific.

  • Our team published our findings in the journal Limnology and Oceanography, titled "Effect of increased CO2 on iron-light-CO2 co-limitation of growth in a marine diatom."

  • Our team collaborate with Professor Luo Yawei published a research paper in the journal Nature Communications titled "Reduced nitrogenase efficiency dominates response of the globally important nitrogen fixer Trichodesmium to ocean acidification."
